Milford Sound Cruise

Milford Sound, located in the Fiordland National Park on the South Island, is one of New Zealand's most popular tourist destinations. A cruise through the sound is a must-do activity, with its towering cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and abundant wildlife. Visitors can choose from a range of cruise options, from scenic nature cruises to more adventurous kayaking and diving trips.

Whale watching in Kaikoura

Kaikoura is a coastal town located on the east coast of the South Island. It's famous for its abundant marine life, including sperm whales, humpback whales, dolphins, and seals. Whale watching tours depart from the town's harbor and take you out into the open ocean where you can see these magnificent creatures up close in their natural habitat.

Glacier hiking in Franz Josef

Franz Josef is a small town located on the west coast of the South Island, famous for its glacier hiking tours. The Franz Josef Glacier is a unique natural wonder, with ice formations that are constantly changing. Hiking on the glacier is a challenging but rewarding experience that allows you to get up close and personal with this incredible natural wonder.

Kayaking in Abel Tasman National Park

Abel Tasman National Park is located in the north of the South Island and is known for its golden sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and lush forests. Kayaking is a popular activity in the park, allowing visitors to explore the coastline and its hidden coves and bays. It's a great way to get close to the local wildlife, including seals, dolphins, and penguins.

Frequently Asked Questions

Some must-see attractions in New Zealand include Milford Sound, the Franz Josef Glacier, Mount Cook National Park, and the Bay of Islands.

The best time to visit New Zealand is between November and April, which is the summer season. This is when the weather is warmer and drier, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

It depends on your nationality. If you're from a visa-waiver country, you can stay in New Zealand for up to 3 months without a visa. If you're not from a visa-waiver country, you'll need to apply for a visitor visa.

The currency in New Zealand is the New Zealand dollar (NZD).

Some popular outdoor activities in New Zealand include hiking, skiing, snowboarding, kayaking, bungee jumping, and skydiving.

New Zealand has a diverse culinary scene, with influences from both Europe and Asia. Some must-try foods include fish and chips, meat pies, and pavlova (a meringue-based dessert).
